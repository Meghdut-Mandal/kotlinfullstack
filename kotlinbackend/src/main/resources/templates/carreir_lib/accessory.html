Accessory Design

Fashion is no longer about apparel and apparel designing. Over the time it has evolved to encompass accessories like bags, belts, jewellery, shoes and much more. This has resulted in the emergence and growth of a specialised sub-domain of the fashion – Accessory design. The field has been an upcoming career among creative minds.


Being one of the most diverse undergraduate design programmes in the country, the accessory design has a well-defined curriculum and teaching pedagogy for grooming future design professionals, training them in the area of integrated expertise in design methodology, materials and production process, consumer behavior and market dynamics, trends and forecast interpretations, business practices and project management in the field of Fashion & Lifestyle Accessories.


Eligibilty Criteria

For pursuing a bachelor’s degree in accessory design, you should have passed class 10+2 qualifying examination from a recognised state or national board of education.

What does accessory design include?


The programme addresses the entire spectrum of accessories and lifestyle products, precious and costume jewellery, leather goods, footwear, watches, giftware, tableware, silverware, crystalware, office furniture, consumer interface design, automobile accessories design, and retail environment design.


Curriculum


Accessory Design has a well-defined curriculum for developing integrated expertise in design methodology, materials and production process, consumer behavior, market dynamics trends, forecast interpretations, business practices and project management in the field of fashion and lifestyle accessories.


Job Profiles


Accessory Design programme prepares students for careers in following capacities in the broad areas of precious and costume jewellery, leather goods, giftware, tableware, watches, footwear, handicrafts, lifestyle products, etc.
