Civil Services

The Civil Service exam is one of the most prestigious career options. Every year, lakhs of students appear in the exam and only the best are able to crack it. The India Civil Services examination is conducted by the UPSC to recruit people to the various civil services, including the Indian Administrative Service, Indian Police, Service, and the Indian Foreign Service. Some of the services that comprise the Group A of the Civil Services are the Indian Revenue Service, Indian Customs and Central Excise Service, Indian Ordinance Factories Service, Indian Postal Service, Indian Civil Accounts Service, Indian Railway Traffic Service, Indian Railway Accounts Service, Railway Protection Force, Indian Defense Estates Service and the Indian Information Service. The other civil service options are the Railway Board secretariat service, Armed forces Headquarters civil service, etc. The Group B Services are the Armed Forces Headquarters Civil Service, Delhi Andaman and Nicobar Island Civil Service, Delhi Andaman and Nicobar Island Civil Service, Pondicherry Civil Service and Pondicherry Police Service.


Entrance Exams for Civil Services

The Civil Services exams are conducted in two stages. The initial stage is the Prelims, which is held to select candidates for the Mains exam. The preliminary examination is an objective paper. Those who clear the preliminary exam have to appear in the Mains, which comprises of written exams. This is followed by an interview. The age limit for appearing in the Civil Service exam is between 21 and 30 years. One has be a graduate in any discipline from a recognized university. For the SC/ST candidates there is a relaxation in the age limit and the number of attempts.

Education

A degree from a Central, State or a Deemed university
A degree received through correspondence or distance education
A degree from an open university
A qualification recognized by the Government of India as being equivalent to one of the above
The following candidates are also eligible, but must submit proof of their eligibility from a competent authority at their institute/university at the time of the main examination, failing which they will not be allowed to attend the exam.
Candidates who have appeared in an examination, the passing of which would render them educationally qualified enough to satisfy one of the above points
Candidates who have passed the final exam of the MBBS degree but have not yet completed an internship.
Candidates who have passed the final exam of ICAI, ICSI and ICWAI.
A degree from a private university.
A degree from any foreign university recognized by the Association of Indian Universities.

Age


The candidate must have attained the age of 21 years and must not have attained the age of 32 years (for the General category candidate) on August 1 of the year of examination. Prescribed age limits vary with respect to caste reservations.
For Other Backward Castes (OBC) the upper age limit is 35 years.
For Scheduled Castes (SC) and Scheduled Tribes (ST), the limit is 37 years.
The upper age limit is relaxed for certain candidates who are backward with respect to other factors and differently abled.
